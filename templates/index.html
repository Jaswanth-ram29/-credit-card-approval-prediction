This tool predicts whether a credit card application is likely to be approved or rejected, based on the same core factors Indian card issuers use: CIBIL score, income, existing debt, and employment history — plus baseline eligibility rules (age, minimum income, minimum CIBIL score) that Indian banks apply before any risk-based scoring.

How it works

  1. Every application first passes a hard eligibility check — minimum age 18, minimum CIBIL score (650), and minimum income by employment type — mirroring real Indian bank policy.
  2. Applicants who clear that gate are then scored by the ML model, trained on applicant profiles and payment histories (60+ days past due = high risk).
  3. Four models are trained — Logistic Regression, Decision Tree, Random Forest, XGBoost — and the best is deployed here.
  4. Every result comes with a risk percentage and the specific factors behind the decision.
